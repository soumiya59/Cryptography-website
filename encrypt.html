Encryption By César

The Caesar cipher works by shifting the letters of the alphabet by a number (key). For example if the key is 3, then there is a distance of 3 characters, so A becomes D.

Encryption By Vegenère

This encryption introduces the notion of key. A key usually comes in the form of a word or phrase. To be able to encrypt our text, at each character we use a letter of the key to perform the substitution.

Encryption By transposition

Transposition cryptography methods are those for which the message is encrypted by permuting the order of the letters of the message according to well-defined rules. In other words, an anagram of the initial message is produced.
